Haridwar - Travel Info

Gateway to the Gods

✨ Spiritual Significance

Haridwar is one of the seven holiest places (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism. It’s believed that drops of Amrit (elixir of immortality) fell here during the Samudra Manthan. Devotees come to take a dip in the holy Ganges at Har Ki Pauri to wash away sins.

📍 Location

Haridwar is located in the state of Uttarakhand, India, at the foothills of the Himalayas where the Ganga River enters the plains.

🗺 Location Map

🚉 How to Reach

  • By Air: Nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport (35 km from Haridwar).
  • By Train: Haridwar Junction is well connected to major cities.
  • By Road: Easily accessible by buses and taxis from Delhi, Rishikesh, Dehradun.

🕒 Temple Timings

Temples and Ghats are open from 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM. Ganga Aarti happens daily at Har Ki Pauri at sunrise and sunset.

📅 Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal due to pleasant weather. Avoid monsoon if you don't like rains or large crowds.

🎉 Major Festivals

  • Kumbh Mela (every 12 years)
  • Ardh Kumbh Mela (every 6 years)
  • Kanwar Mela
  • Diwali & Kartik Purnima

📝 Tips for Visitors

  • Attend Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri – it's a breathtaking experience.
  • Wear modest clothes; it's a sacred place.
  • Be cautious while bathing in the Ganges – the current is strong.

📍 Nearby Attractions

  • Rishikesh (24 km)
  • Mansa Devi Temple
  • Chandi Devi Temple
  • Bharat Mata Mandir

🛕 Why Visit Haridwar?

Haridwar offers a unique spiritual atmosphere where the Ganga flows pure and sacred. It's a perfect blend of ancient traditions, temple rituals, and serene riverbanks. Pilgrims come to seek salvation, witness the powerful Ganga Aarti, and explore the divine energy of this holy city.
